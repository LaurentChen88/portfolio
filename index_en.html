Feb.2024 - Mar.2024. CAPI stands for Computer Assisted Personal Interviewing. In other words, it is a method of data collection that takes place face-to-face, with the interviewer using a tablet to fill out the form and the interviewee providing responses. Socio is the partner of the Louvre for conducting surveys on Louvre visitors. Specifically, the interviewers conduct interviews using our form. Then, the data team processes and analyzes the data. The aim of the surveys is to better understand the visitors to attract more of them. However, it is not possible to conduct interviews without an internet connection. This is a problem because some areas may have poor or no connection at all. This causes us to lose time, interviews, and potentially representativeness. I'm here to solve this problem, the goal is to implement a feature enabling connectless interviews. To achieve this, I use the local storage of the browser to save the responses.
Additionally, I use PWA technology to transform our questionnaire into an application. I code in JavaScript, HTML, and CSS. After testing numerous approaches, I created a page that contains the interview table, with options to resume an interview, delete it, and send it to the server. This project allowed me to surpass myself, as I learned to code in Javascript, and to strengthen my ability to search and debug.

University projects

May 2024. With a group of three people, I conducted PCA and classifications, following an exploratory analysis, on football player data. The goal is to reduce the number of variables and to group the observations into clusters.
First, I performed the data cleaning. To conduct a good analysis of the players over the season, I chose to retain only those who participated significantly. Specifically, this means focusing on players who played more than 5 matches and had an average playing time of more than 45 minutes per match. To facilitate our analysis, I decided to consolidate the player positions into a single position per player: defender (DF), forward (FW), midfielder (MF), or goalkeeper (G).
Here is an example of PCA with axes 1 and 2:

The variables that contribute the most to axis 1 are SCA, PasAss, Shots, SoT, GCA, ToAtt, PPA, CPA. These variables are all related to offensive play, such as forwards. We can say that players on the right of the circle have an offensive play style, while players on the left of the circle have a less offensive play style.
The variables that contribute the most to axis 2 are Rec, Touches, PasAtt, Carries. These variables are strongly related to a style of play that involves a lot of ball control, such as midfielders. We can say that players at the top of the circle are those who control the ball a lot, while players at the bottom of the circle control the ball less.
However, it was not always easy to determine the number of clusters to keep.

I initially wanted to keep 6 clusters. But in reality, 3 clusters were enough, as we cut the tree where there is the largest jump.
I performed the processing and analyses using R. I used the explor package, which facilitates our study in the context of PCA. The output is an HTML file, generated with Rmarkdown.
This project greatly enhanced my teamwork skills and analytical capacity.

Feb.2024 - Mar.2024. I conducted web data collection.
Using the SIRENE database data.gouv.fr, my goal was to complete the information with geographical coordinates (latitude, longitude) via the address API, and other information (phone number, website) obtained via a Google Maps search, using web scraping. I worked on the "StockEtablissement" file, which has over 38 million rows and 38 columns. I also used the "StockUniteLegale" file with over 26 million rows and 34 columns. I manipulated the data by coding it in Python using libraries like pandas, Beautiful Soup, Selenium. In the end, I delivered the Notebook and a short report explaining the process and the difficulties encountered.
The files are very large, so they need to be read and loaded block by block. I first tested with 1000 rows to make sure that it works before moving on to more rows.
When concatenating variables to get the address or the name of the establishment, there are often missing values. I then removed them. I only kept the variables of interest to optimize the process.
The searches via the address API do not always gives results. I left these observations without longitude and latitude. A pause is needed after each call to the address API because the number of requests per second is limited.
This project was not easy, as I encountered many of the previously mentioned issues. Finding information through web scraping was the hardest part. But, I managed to retrieve the phone number and website for some observations.

As a potential improvement, I believe optimizing the program as much as possible is a real advantage for it to run faster and more efficiently.

Dec. 2024. In a team of two, I analyzed a time series.
As part of a forecast study using data provided by the EIA (Energy Information Administration), the objective was to understand geothermal energy production from 2001 to the end of 2022 and to make predictions for the year 2023. The deliverable was a report.

The series is additive, with an irregular upward trend. These irregular variations caused by COVID-19 are explained by the trend curve and especially by the residuals. Outliers could explain unexpected variations. We observed a seasonal pattern that repeats each year.

I used three forecasting methods as indicated above. These three predictions are different from each other. However, they all have the same trend. After adjusting our predictions, we found that the Holt-Winters method is the best, as its prediction curve better fits the actual data compared to the other methods, and its mean squared error is the lowest.
This project allowed me to understand the specifics of time series data analysis.

Feb.2023-Jun.2023. With my group of 4 people, I thought about a problem based on the data made available to me. This study is based on data from a database provided by the Observational Study in Clinical Epidemiology of 5 US hospitals that applied the RHC method. It concerns 5,735 patients over 18 years of age hospitalized in intensive care between June 1989 and January 1994. RHC, or right heart catheterization, consists in placing a Swan Ganz catheter to resuscitate a patient in serious condition. I wanted to see if this treatment, applied to intensive care patients, was beneficial, if it could save the most people, and more specifically to see if the RHC method was relevant and necessary in certain situations.
The goal was to produce a technical report, a statistical report, a project management report, and a final presentation of the results with slides. The technical report contains the approach I took on R to clean and sort our data. Indeed, it was necessary to understand and assimilate the data. After cleaning, my observations went from 5735 patients to 5508 patients. For example, I removed observations that had outliers on a variable. I decided to delete a variable that had a lot of missing values. I also grouped variables into one for easier manipulation later on, etc.
Then I made graphs and analyses where I added it to the statistical report. And I presented our results on Powerpoint during the oral presentation. Throughout the project, I drew up a project management report, which consisted in planning the project with a Gantt chart, organizing it, and seeing whether the expected time and resources had been respected.
I used R software for data processing and analysis. This project taught me a lot about teamwork, organization and autonomy.
